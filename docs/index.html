Individual Session: Recovering Dot/Cross Product and Shadow-Area Interpretation

One-on-one session reconstructing u·v = |u||v|cos θ and the cross product as a determinant with i,j,k symbols, then interpreting each component of u×v as the signed shadow area of the parallelogram on the corresponding coordinate plane.

Cayley–Hamilton via Cyclic Basis: Existence Gap and Four Proof Strategies

Recaps the algebraic Cayley–Hamilton proof using (λI-M)·adj(λI-M) = det(λI-M)·I, then resumes the cyclic-basis proof, identifies the existence-of-cyclic-basis gap, and previews four distinct proof strategies.

Solving Linear Recursions by Diagonalizing the Companion Matrix; Nilpotent Intro

Reformulates a second-order linear recursion as first-order vector recursion aₙ₊₁ = M·aₙ, solves by diagonalizing the companion matrix, and introduces the nilpotent block for repeated eigenvalue.

Powering Jordan Blocks: Binomial Expansion with Nilpotent N

Continues from morning by explicitly powering (λI+N)^n for 2×2 and generalizing to k×k Jordan blocks, showing that the binomial expansion produces the polynomial-in-n coefficient predicted by the old algebraic theory.
