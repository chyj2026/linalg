Hyperbola Geometry: Foci, Asymptotes, and c² = a² + b²

Published

October 4, 2025

Lead

Derives focal relation \(c^2 = a^2 + b^2\) for hyperbola by multiple methods.

Theorems

(Hyperbola). \(x^2/a^2 - y^2/b^2 = 1\), foci \((\pm c, 0)\): \(||PF_1| - |PF_2|| = 2a\), \(c^2 = a^2 + b^2\). \(\tag{89}\)

(Asymptotes). \(y = \pm (b/a)x\). (Latus rectum). \(y = b^2/a\).

Derivation

\(x^2/a^2 - y^2/b^2 = 1\); \(|x|\to\infty\): \(y \approx \pm(b/a)x\). Focal property: \(|F_1V - F_2V| = 2a\) giving \(c^2 = a^2+b^2\) via asymptote triangle. \(\quad\blacksquare\)

Verification audit

  • \(a=b=1\). \(c=\sqrt 2\), asymptotes \(y=\pm x\). ✓
  • Toby’s \(b\to ib\) converts hyperbola to ellipse. Anticipates Feb 21 afternoon.
